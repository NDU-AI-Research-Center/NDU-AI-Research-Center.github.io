About Us

The AI Research Center of the Ndejje University (NDU) is dedicated to deliver state-of-the-art AI driven solutions to local challenges in Uganda and its communities. It is facilitated by the NDU's faculty of Science and Computing in cooperation with the christian German-based organization Coworkers.

Research Directions

Machine Learning in Energy Grid Stability

We do research to apply machine learning approaches to optimize power distribution, reduce energy wastage, and ensure reliable electricity supply, contributing to economic development and improved living standards in the country.

AI in Education

We aim to create innovative solution of AI in education that hold the potential to revolutionize Uganda's education system by providing personalized learning experiences, improving teacher-student ratios, and enhancing educational access and quality across the country.

Chatbot-based Service Delivery

We research on Chatbots that overcome geographical barriers, increase accessibility to essential services, and enhance the efficiency and convenience of public service interactions, thereby improving the overall quality of life for Ugandan citizens.

Current Prototypes

Our UMEME Power Outage Duration Prediction predicts how long it will take for a reported Power Outage Alert to be fixed. Predictions are made using a Neural Network trained on 4 weeks of webscraped data form UMEME homepage. Test-set Accuracy is at 95%, which results in an prediciton error that is in 80% of the cases below one hour. Currently we are evaluating the accuracy on unseen data.
